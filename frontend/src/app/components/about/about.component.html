About Me

Hi, I'm Colten — a Computer Science Ph.D. student at Purdue University and a Presidential Fellow in the Freedom Research Lab, advised by Prof. Aniket Kate. My research centers on applied cryptography: secure multiparty computation, anti-collusion mechanisms, and asynchronous distributed protocols.

Before Purdue, I earned my B.S. in Computer Engineering from the University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign, where I built cryptographic protocols for privacy-preserving healthcare and interned at Synchrony Financial on PKI and cloud security. I enjoy the full arc of the work — from proving a protocol correct to shipping the code that runs it.

Whether it's a garbled circuit, a zero-knowledge proof, or an embedded system, I like building things that are provably secure and genuinely useful. A few technologies I work with:

  • Python
  • Rust
  • C / C++
  • SystemVerilog
  • Secure MPC
  • Zero-Knowledge Proofs
