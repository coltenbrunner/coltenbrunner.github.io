About

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Purdue University, where I am supported by a Presidential Fellowship. My work centers on the design and analysis of cryptographic and distributed protocols — in particular, understanding how rational, self-interested behavior affects the security of multiparty computation and consensus protocols.

Before Purdue, I completed a B.S. in Computer Engineering at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, where I worked with the Decentralized Systems Laboratory on applied multiparty computation.
